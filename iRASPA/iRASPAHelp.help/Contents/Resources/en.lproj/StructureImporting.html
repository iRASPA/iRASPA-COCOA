Structure importing

The project-navgiator has 3 sections: GALLERY, LOCAL PROJECTS, and ICLOUD PUBLIC. The gallery contains some examples to play around with, in "local projects" you can put your own projects with editting capability, and the icloud-part contains cloud-stored databases. The gallery and cloud sections are immutable, but can be dragged and dropped or copy-and-pasted to your "local projects"-section, after which they can be editted for content and appearance.

You can view and edit your own structures by importing them. The most common formats for structures are CIF-files and PDB-files. The easiest way of importing these types of files is to drag them from the Finder and then drop them in the project-navigator in the "local projects"-section. Alternatively, you can use the menu options "File" and then "Import".

Structures are published in chemical information files like PDB, CIF, XYZ, etc. Each format has its specific (dis-)advantages. Biomolecules like 1AON are usually in PDB-format. The 1AON protein consists of 21 subunit chains that are not connected (indicated by the TER-recordtype. Such a structure would be imported into iRASPA as a structure-project with 21 movies consisting of one frame. The Scene-navigator shows the 21 units lists as movies in one scene (see picture below).

The MODEL/ENDMDL PDB-records are used for multiple frames and hence often used for movies. The individual frames can be viewed using the Frame-navigator.

The proteint PDB-files usually also contain solvent atoms. In iRASPA the visibility of movies can be individually switch on and off, which quickly shows which subunit is the solvent. In the Scene-navigator the solvent-movie can dragged to the empty canvas at the bottom of the navigator to form its own scene (see figure below for the hemoglobin biomolecule). When exporting as a PDB-file, the two scenes will be written to separate files.
