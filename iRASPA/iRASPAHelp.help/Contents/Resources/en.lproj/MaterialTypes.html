Material types

iRASPA uses material types to distinguish between various behaviours. For example, crystals are periodic and hence have a unit cell. For atomic crystals, the most natural coordinate is the fractional position. In contrast, a molecule is nonperiodic, has no unit-cell, and therefore must be defined in Cartesian coordinates.

iRASPA therefore defines and uses the following material types:

Crystal

A crystal uses fractional positions, is periodic, has a unit cell, a space group, and is viewed as a collection of individual atoms. Atoms are confined to the unit cell and bonds sticking out of the unit cell are cut at the unit cell interface.

Molecular crystal

A molecular crystal uses Cartesian positions, is periodic, has a unit cell, a space group, and is viewed as a collection of molecules.

Protein crystal

A protein crystal uses Cartesian positions, is periodic, has a unit cell, a space group, and is viewed as a collection of atoms. More than 50% of all atoms belong to aminoacids. Atoms are not placed back in the unit ell by default.

Molecule

A molecule uses Cartesian positions, is non-periodic, has no unit cell, no space group, and is viewed as a collection of atoms. Althought there is no unit cell, there is a bounding-box.

Protein

A protein uses Cartesian positions, is non-periodic, has no unit cell, no space group, and is viewed as a collection of atoms. Althought there is no unit cell, there is a bounding-box. More than 50% of all atoms belong to aminoacids.
