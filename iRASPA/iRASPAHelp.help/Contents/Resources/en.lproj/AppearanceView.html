Appearance-view

In the appearance-view, the looks and feel of atoms, bonds, unit cells, and adsorption surfaces in the scene can be controlled. The appearances can be viewed and set for multiple structures simultaneously in three ways:

  1. Using the project navigator.

    2. All frames of all movies of all scenes are used as the target for viewing and changing properties.

  2. Using the scene navigator for individual settings per movie.

    3. All frames of all selected movies of all scenes are used as the target for viewing and changing properties.

  3. Using the frame navigator for individual settings per frame.

    4. All selected frames of the selected movie are used as the target for viewing and changing properties.

Atoms

Atoms can be drawn using a variety of looks, (1) a default-look based on ball-and-stick, (2) a fancy-style with Van der Waals sized atoms, ambient occlusion, and half-desaturated colors, and (3) a licorice-style. The selection style of atoms comes in three flavors: (1) transparent Worley 3D noise, (2) striped yellow sphere, and (3) glowing effect. Whether a particular style works well depends on the setting. Option (1) seems to work (reasonably) well for all settings and is therefore the default.

Bonds

Bonds can be drawn in various styles. The "uniform" style uses a uniform color that can be selected from a color-well (Bond lighting). The "split" and "gradient"-style color the bond as a split or gradient between the atom colors making up the bond taking the relative sizes of the atoms into account.

Unit cell

The unit cell drawing can be switched off and on. The width of the tubes can be set, and the lighting, color, and intensity can be controlled.

Adsorption surface

Adsorption surfaces visualize the energy landscape of a crystals. The landscape depends on the probe molecule and the force field. The computation consists of two parts: (1) the computation of an energy grid, (2) 2D surface extraction from the 3D energy grid. The energy grid is recomputed when the probe molecule is changed. Surface extraction is fast and different isovalues can be selected interactively using a slider (or textfield). The lighting of the front and back of the surface can be edited, as well as the opacity.

Text annotation

Atoms can be annotated with text. The font can selected from a pull-down menu and the text can be scaled in size. Alignment options include combinations of: left, right, top, and bottom. The text will always face the camera, but can be occluded by other objects in the scene (e.g. the text is placed in 3D just in front of the atom like a "flat billboard"). The occlusion is natural in 3D and makres front atoms standout. However, sometimes the occlusion is undesired, for example when bonds intersect the text. Therefore, the text can be offset in x,y,z. Increasing the z-value by 0.5 moves the text 0.5 an Angstrom closer to the camera.
